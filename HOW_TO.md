# How to do a site survey

For anyone doing a site visit with this system. No prior knowledge assumed. Look at the sample job (Karen & David Mitchell) first — it shows what a finished survey looks like.

## Before you leave

1. Open Spa Jobs on your phone **while on the office/home wifi** (the icon on the home screen, or ask for the address — it looks like `http://192.168.x.x:4321`). Opening it on wifi pre-loads every job so the app keeps working on site with no signal — anything you enter out there syncs back automatically later. Photos, sketch, measurements and notes all work offline; only video/scan uploads wait for a connection.
2. Find the customer under **Customers**. If they're new, add them: name, phone, address.
3. Open their job. If there's no job yet, start one from their page: short title and install type.
4. Check the **next action** on the job says you're doing the survey today.

## On site — do it in this order

Open the job, tap **Start site survey**.

1. **Walk the whole route first, phone away.** Gate to spa position. Note in your head: the narrowest point, steps, anything overhead.

2. **Photos.** Tap **+ Add photo**. Take at least:
   - the spa position from two angles
   - every pinch point on the access route (gate, side path, corners)
   - the switchboard, and the nearest tap and drain
   - anything in the way: trees, sheds, AC units, retaining walls
   Give each photo a one-line caption. Then tap the photo and **draw on it**: box where the spa goes, line for the access route, line for pipe or cable runs. Red pen, doesn't need to be neat.

3. **Video walkthrough.** Film one continuous walk from the street to the spa position and **talk the whole time**: where it's going, distances, what the ground is like, how the shell gets in, anything dangerous, and what the customer said they want. Upload it and tap **Transcribe video** (takes a few minutes — keep the page open; the very first one on a new computer takes longer). Then tap **Sort transcript into notes** and read every box — fix anything it misheard. If you'd rather not film, type your notes in the transcript box and sort them the same way.

4. **Sketch.** Draw the yard from above: house, fences, gate, spa position, and the route in. Mark pipe and cable runs if you know them. Tap **Save sketch**.

5. **Measurements.** Tape measure, not guesses:
   - spa length, width, depth, dry weight (off the spec sheet or ask the dealer)
   - for in-ground digs: the excavation size — dig length, width and depth (the hole, not the spa — allow for the base and working room). The system works out the spoil volume and how many bogie truck loads to allow
   - the concrete slab: length, width and thickness — the system works out the cubic metres of concrete
   - any retaining walls: type (timber sleepers, concrete sleepers, Dincel, Besser block...), length, height, thickness
   - any decking: length, width and the brand/product they want
   - plumbing: tick if an external drainage pit is needed, and note where it ties in
   - electrical: supply size (15 A or 32 A) and the cable run length from the switchboard
   - distance from the spa position to: rear boundary, side boundary, house wall, switchboard, nearest tap, sewer point
   - the **narrowest** point of the access route
   - steps and level changes on the route

6. **Site conditions.** Ground type, slope, obstacles, any visible services (meters, pits, overhead lines). Tick **crane needed** if the shell can't be walked in — when in doubt, tick it and say why in the machinery notes.

7. **3D scan (big jobs only).** If the job justifies it, scan the yard with the Scaniverse or Polycam app — slow, steady passes, 5-10 minutes — export as GLB and upload it on the survey. The 3D view can then show the spa in the customer's actual yard.

8. **Customer wishlist and budget.** Write down what they asked for in their words, and any budget figure they mentioned.

9. **Save.** Tap **Save + mark survey done** before you drive off. If the signal's bad, plain **Save survey** works offline to the office computer as soon as you're back on the wifi.

## Why this matters

The quote is priced from your measurements. The contractor spec sheets — what the concreter, plumber and sparky get instead of a phone call — are built from your photos, sketch and notes. If you don't capture it, someone has to go back. **A second site visit costs more than ten careful minutes on the first one.**
